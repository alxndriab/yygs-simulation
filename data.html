School

They say that children are the future. And it is thanks to the advancements in technology that humanity continues to evolve their education for students. Education-focused unions and organizations have been developing since the Antebellum Era. And each year, the new devices, techniques, digitalization, and forms of communication and work progress into more efficient and creative-stimulating works to engage the future creators. Here are a few monumental technologies.

Example of analyzing data of Covid-19 death rates in the United States


For an idea of how data can be so helpful for so many here’s an example in the COVID-19 pandemic, “...multiple new experiments in data gathering and analysis have sprung up during the pandemic, experiments that might save thousands of lives before the crisis is over. And they may well prevent future pandemics from developing in the first place”. And they soon found that the more data the better. “By early February, the Open Covid-19 Data Working Group had assembled detailed records for 10,000 cases. Today an informal network of hundreds of volunteers has assembled records for more than a million cases in 142 countries around the world. It may well be the single most accurate portrait of the virus’s spread through the human population in existence”. This is huge. The better the accuracy, the more likely they can find a solution that can make an impact. And with all the samples, they cast such a wide net that they can see how people were affected from all over. This is basically a scientist's dream to have this much data. “Of course, the greatest value in that kind of data set lies in the clues it can give us about the future path of the disease and how that path can potentially be interrupted”.

Conclusions: With this data, scientists were able to draw conclusions such as, “Life tables are what allowed us to see that Covid-19’s lethality has been disproportionately concentrated among the elderly, unlike the flu pandemic of 1918, which killed an unusual number of young adults”.

(Source: New York Times Magazine)

Educational Programs

Parents often say that TV isn’t educational and will melt your brain, figuratively of course. But that isn’t true. Today we have many educational channels like PBS and National Geographic. But it was much earlier that this began. The Ford Foundation was important in the creation of educational TV. In the 1950s, television started to be considered for a teaching device, even setting aside 242 educational channels”. Although later in the 1980s it lost traction and funding thanks to the highly favored computers. “U.S. Commissioner of Education Earl James McGrath, declared, ‘Through the use of television, educational institutions will be able to bring the greatest teachers, the finest artists, scientists, and philosophers into schools and homes.’”

(Source: Encyclopedia.com)

3D Printers

In 2020, with the influx of advancements in technology, there is an increasing need of engineers. Technology like the 3D printer can help with the education & development of trade skills. “...we also need new education and training programs to raise a new generation of industrial designers".

(Source: Forbes)

Augmented Reality

AR has made great strides in recent years, and therefore its abilities in education have too. “Autodesk has recently been 3D scanning the historic Apollo 11 lunar module, which has slowly been degrading over time. Capturing that data allows us to preserve a digital copy of the Apollo 11 — but more importantly it allows us to share it with others for educational purposes. When combined with AR and VR capabilities, this means students in classrooms around the country — and around the world — can virtually step into the Apollo 11 and experience it for themselves".

(Source: Autodesk)

Virtual Reality

VR has the capability to technically transport you into a new world which helps engagement in a lesson thanks to the virtual hands-on interaction. It helps students by providing an immersive, interactive experience. For one, it has helped students with autism and other learning difficulties, “A 2007 study showed that children with autism could effectively apply skills they had learned in a virtual environment — like fire and street/road safety — to the real world”. And VR can also help with social and communication skills. A start-up called Floreo uses VR technology to help students with autism practice interacting with others and more.

(Source: CB Insights)

Gameification

Games are at least one of kids’ favorite things, and gamification has brought gaming into education. An app called Duolingo utilizes gaming to teach languages. A feature of duolingo is its “timed task-based game” with rewards and varying levels. However, there is some debate about how gamification really impacts students, “While educators are likely to focus on how gamification keeps students engaged and self-motivated, critics point out that embedding entertainment into learning can make students more easily distracted”. Still though, it has definitely impacted the way we learn. And although we don’t know its long-term effects, - if used right it - it provides a nice break from regular school work.

(Source: CB Insights)

Learning Platforms

As is common knowledge, 2020 has totally changed education. So, education had to adapt. And in that adaption came the spectacular rise of learning platforms like Canvas. Canvas is similar to google classroom - an organization tool for students; for assignments, announcements, classes, etc.

Artificial Intelligence

“The education industry is experimenting with artificial intelligence (AI) applications…” AI can also prove helpful for students in overcoming language barriers in education. For example, Microsoft’s Presentation Translator is an AI tool that provides real-time subtitles for presentations and lectures. Students can hear or read these in their native language.

(Source: CB Insights)

Online Communication

In 2020 through early 2021, students have been forced to stay home for the most part. And the ways students have connected have changed. It is now through online video calls, like Zoom. To put it simply, apps like Zoom skyrocketed. This is shown when Zoom went from 10 million daily users in December of 2020 to over 300 million daily users around April of 2020. And it is still being used daily in 2021. Needless to say, online communication like Zoom plays a heavy role in keeping the school community connected.

(Source: The Verge)

In summation, there have been a lot more technical advancements in education in recent years, especially with the current pandemic. Today, variety is insane. However, inventions in our grandparents’ time shouldn’t be overlooked. They were the foundation of today’s inventions. For instance the beginning of educational programs helped support other ways of education besides just school. It was so new to think that TV could teach, yet today even video game technology like AR, VR and gamification can be found in use for education!
